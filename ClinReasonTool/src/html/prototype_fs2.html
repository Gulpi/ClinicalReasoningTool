Short n' SIMPLE - 65-year-old man with cough - Mr. Johnston


You and Dr. Carter greet Mr. Johnston and ask him the following questions:
"Tell me a little bit about this cough, did you have cold symptoms before the cough started?"
"Well actually I had a cough off and on for a few years. But it went away when I stopped smoking a couple of years ago. The cough I have now has been going on for a little over two months."

"Did you have cold symptoms before the cough started?"
"I didn't notice any cold symptoms at the start."

"Have you had a runny nose or sore throat with the cough?"
"No, nothing like that."

"Have you been taking any medications, including the inhaler you have?"
"Just using my inhaler like always."

"Does anything seem to make the cough better or worse?"
"My inhaler helps a little, but I am still coughing quite a bit."

"When you cough, do you bring anything up?"
"My girlfriend said I should come in because of the blood I've noticed sometimes when I cough. The blood comes up with a deep cough, and is sometimes mixed with mucous, and it seems to come and go."

"Tell me a little more about the blood you are coughing up."
"It is maybe a half teaspoon once or twice a week of blood."

"Are you having any other symptoms, like shortness of breath?"
"I've been able to work even with the cough, although I have not been able to climb stairs as quickly as usual as I've been too tired."

"Have you had a fever?"
"No."

"Have you lost weight?"
"Not that I've noticed, but my girlfriend keeps mentioning that my pants are a little loose."

"Have you ever been tested for tuberculosis?"
"Not that I know of."

On review of systems, he has not had any chills, or chest pain, but he thinks he notices some wheezing occasionally.
